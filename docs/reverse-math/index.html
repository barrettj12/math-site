Reverse mathematics

Reverse mathematics has been the topic of my Honours project and my Masters thesis. Informally, reverse mathematics studies what axioms are required to prove certain theorems of “ordinary mathematics”. The Wikipedia page gives a nice summary; otherwise, for a more in-depth exposition, see my Honours report, or the books by Simpson or Stillwell.

My Honours project was under the supervision of Rod Downey and Noam Greenberg. We studied the reverse mathematical strength of Cousin’s lemma, a compactness principle arising in the study of the gauge integral.

Briefly, a gauge is a positive valued function \delta\colon [0,1] \to \mathbb{R}, and a tagged partition is a finite sequence

P = \langle 0 = x_0 < t_0 < x_1 < t_1 < \cdots < x_{\ell-1} < t_{\ell-1} < x_\ell = 1 \rangle

A partition P is \delta-fine if the open balls B \big( t_j, \delta(t_j) \big) cover [0,1] .

Cousin’s lemma. Every gauge \delta\colon [0,1] \to \mathbb{R} has a \delta-fine partition.

Reverse mathematics is traditionally done in second-order arithmetic, which is not powerful enough to talk about arbitrary functions f\colon \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}. However, it can talk about any function which can be specified by countable information—examples include the continuous functions, Baire functions, and Borel functions. Rod, Noam and I proved the following results about Cousin’s lemma:

Theorem (Barrett, Downey, Greenberg). All implications are over \mathsf{RCA}_0.

  • Cousin’s lemma for continuous functions is equivalent to \mathsf{WKL}_0.
  • Cousin’s lemma for Baire 1 functions is equivalent \mathsf{ACA}_0.
  • Cousin’s lemma for Borel functions, or Baire n functions (n \geq 2) is equivalent to \mathsf{ATR}_0 (modulo some induction).

These results can be found in our joint paper, and some were included earlier in my Honours thesis.

My Masters thesis is under the supervision of Dan Turetsky. We have been studying the reverse mathematics of certain topics in ring theory, particularly Noetherian rings and integral domains.

There are many equivalent definitions of Noetherian: Sato gave eight in his PhD thesis. We’ve conducted a full reverse-mathematical analysis of these definitions, first narrowing it down to four equivalent definitions. One of these equivalences uses a “moving marker” priority argument, and we seem to need \mathsf{I}\Sigma^0_2 to prove that it’s correct. Hence, we instead work over a base theory of \mathsf{RCA}_0 + \mathsf{I}\Sigma^0_2.

With these four notions, \mathsf{ACA}_0 proves that they are all equivalent. We have obtained reversals showing that several of the equivalences actually need \mathsf{ACA}_0 or \mathsf{WKL}_0, and so have totally classified the axiomatic strength of almost all the equivalences.

We’ve also looked at integral domains, particularly PIDs and UFDs. Classically, there are several equivalent definitions of PIDs:

Theorem. For an integral domain R, the following are equivalent:

  1. Every ideal I \subseteq R is principal.
  2. Every prime ideal I \subseteq R is principal.
  3. R admits a Dedekind–Hasse norm: a function f\colon R \to \mathbb{N} such that
    • f(r) = 0 \iff r = 0_R
    • For all nonzero a, b \in R, either b \mid a or there exist x,y \in R such that 0 < f(ax + by) < f(b).
    • For all nonzero a, b \in R, f(a) \leq f(ab).

We actually take “ideal” to mean “\Sigma_1-ideal” instead of “\Delta_1-ideal”, as this seems to be the right notion in systems weaker than \mathsf{ACA}_0. We’ve got a proof of (1)\Leftrightarrow(2) in \mathsf{ACA}_0 – this is nontrivial, since the usual proof uses Zorn’s lemma! We’ve also shown that (1)\Leftrightarrow(3) is equivalent to \mathsf{ACA}_0.

Another nice result we have is a construction of a computable PID in which the set of primes/irreducibles is \Pi^0_2-complete. Dzhafarov and Mileti did this for UFDs, but we needed a totally different construction for PIDs. All of these results (and many others) are included in my Masters thesis, and will also feature in some upcoming papers based on this work.
